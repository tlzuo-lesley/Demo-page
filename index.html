XEmoRAG: Cross-Lingual Emotion Transfer with Controllable Intensity Using Retrieval-Augmented Generation



Abstract

Zero-shot emotion transfer in cross-lingual speech synthesis refers to generating speech in a target language, where the emotion is expressed based on reference speech from a different source language.However, this task remains challenging due to the scarcity of parallel multilingual emotional corpora, the presence of foreign accent artifacts, and the difficulty of separating emotion from language-specific prosodic features.In this work, we explore a practical yet underexplored scenario: transferring emotions from Chinese to Thai in a zero-shot setting without relying on parallel emotional data. We propose XEmoRAG, a novel framework for cross-lingual emotion transfer with controllable intensity, using retrieval-augmented generation to guide emotional expression.XEmoRAG extracts language-agnostic emotional embeddings from a Chinese reference using a pre-trained emotion encoder and retrieves emotionally matched Thai utterances from a curated emotional database. These retrieved prompts guide generation through a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipeline. Additionally, a flow-matching alignment module minimizes pitch and duration mismatches to ensure natural prosody and speaker timbre blending.Experimental results show that XEmoRAG synthesizes expressive and natural Thai speech using only Chinese reference audio, without requiring explicit emotion labels.These findings demonstrate the effectiveness of XEmoRAG in controllable and data-efficient cross-lingual emotion transfer, making it well-suited for multilingual movie dubbing, especially in low-resource scenarios.
